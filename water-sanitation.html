Project Overview

For ESC204 Praxis III, our eight-person team was challenged to prototype a solution to a real-world access-to-clean-water problem. We designed and built an automated water sanitation system that heats water to a specified pasteurization temperature, holds it there for a defined duration, and then automatically dispenses the treated water into a potable water tank — no manual intervention needed after the cycle starts.

The system integrates a DS18B20 temperature sensor, a relay-controlled heating element, and a motor-driven dispensing mechanism — all coordinated by a microcontroller running custom Arduino firmware. All structural components were designed in Fusion 360 and fabricated via 3D printing. Project coordination and documentation were managed in Notion, and artifacts are mirrored in this repository for direct public access.

This page hosts all project files for public access — use the file browser below to explore reports, CAD models, source code, and meeting notes.

How It Works

01

Fill

Raw water is loaded into the heating chamber via the inlet, either manually or through a valve.

02

Heat

The heating element raises the water to 70 °C, monitored continuously by the temperature sensor.

03

Hold

Water is maintained at pasteurization temperature for 30 seconds to fully inactivate pathogens.

04

Dispense

A motor-driven mechanism automatically dispenses the safe, treated water into a clean storage tank.
